# Embermane — The Fox Whose Fire Never Dies

---

## At a Glance

- **Animal form:** Silver fox — fur like moonlight, eyes like old winter. Graceful, gleaming, with the confidence of someone who has been watching for a very long time.
- **Human name:** Mira Cinderglen — bakery-keeper of Hollowroot, whose hearth warmed half the Vale, whose kindness was legend.
- **Role in the Covenant:** Warmth and fire. The one whose heart burned into her animal form.
- **Location:** A woodland shrine southeast of Timberhearth, through tangled underbrush

---

## Who Embermane Is

Mira Cinderglen saw the rot in her flour before she knew what it was. She felt the wrongness in the dough. She came to the Hollowroot Tree and she stood. Seven centuries later, she holds the line at a shrine that is slowly being consumed by blight — stone arches half-sunken into moss, lilies turning dark at the tips, vines threading through the roots.

She is sly, warm, and quietly amused. She addresses Gabriel and Jessica as *"my slightly less-clumsy apprentices."*

---

## The Encounter

✅ **[CANON]** Gabriel and Jessica arrived at her shrine in a homemade deer costume (front and back). Poodler accompanied them, barking the entire way. The costume had already lost a hoof and one antler by the time they arrived.

Mira emerged from the shadows: *"Oh my. What a rare and majestic creature wanders into my grove tonight."* And then: *"I know what you are, children. You're not here for disguise. You're here for me."*

---

## The Trial of Balance

✅ **[CANON]** Mira set them a trial — a narrow bridge of woven light and shadow across a void of writhing vines. One carried light (Jessica). One carried shadow (Gabriel). They had to walk in perfect harmony, matching pace and breath.

They stumbled. They argued about which left. The bridge groaned. Then they reached out — hands clasping, breath matching — and walked together. The bridge held. They crossed.

*"You kept balance,"* she said. *"Even in fear. Even in doubt. That is rare."*

---

## Why She Couldn't Leave

✅ **[CANON]** Mira could not yet leave her post. The blight had grown strong around her shrine, and she alone was holding it back. If she left, Timberhearth would suffer.

*"You must return to me later, when the path is clearer."*

This is an open thread. One possibility: once Ringtail's condition is resolved, or once enough Guardians are reconnected, the weight redistributes and Mira can move.
