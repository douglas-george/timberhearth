# The Unknown Eight — Guardians Not Yet Found

🔒 **[HIDDEN]** — This entire document is DM-only planning material.

The players know these eight names from the Tome of Hollowroot Fragment. They do not know where to find them, what condition they are in, or what each encounter might require.

---

## Stoneback — The Badger

**Human name:** Bramm the Forge-Hand — spoke little, shaped sigils into steel, saw his anvil crack without cause.
**Nature:** Unyielding. Stubborn. Perhaps bitterly so, after centuries.

*DM notes: Where is Stoneback? In an old forge? Deep in the hills? Is he bitter or still steadfast? An encounter with Stoneback could test Gabriel's patience and persistence rather than cleverness.*

---

## Mothshade — The Moth

**Human name:** Sister Aldith — veiled, soft-spoken, read the moon, dreamed of vines choking the stars.
**Nature:** Between waking and dream. She may not be entirely of the physical world anymore.

*DM notes: Mothshade could be the most otherworldly encounter — dream-based, disorienting, poetic. She may need to be reached at night, in a half-awake state, through something like a dream-walk.*

---

## Hearthgnaw — The Squirrel

**Human name:** Torren Wex — carpenter with laughter in his lungs, found his ladders snapped overnight.
**Nature:** Bold, acrobatic, and probably chaotic to find. May be in the treetops, the buildings, the woodwork of Timberhearth itself.

*DM notes: A fun, energetic encounter. Hearthgnaw might be the most playful find — visible but quick, needing to be charmed or matched in energy rather than solemnly approached.*

---

## Bramblegut — The Boar

**Human name:** Thalia Grimbrow — boisterous tavern-keeper who arm-wrestled a bear (or so she claimed). Poured the first cup of binding mead.
**Nature:** Strong, joyous — her laughter now an echo through the roots. May be in the wild woods beyond Timberhearth.

*DM notes: Bramblegut could be a feast-encounter — a challenge of hospitality, generosity, or physical courage. She was a tavern-keeper; she responds to warmth, food, and genuine boldness.*

---

## Mistpelt — The Deer

**Human name:** Unknown.
**Nature:** Grace and wildness combined. Deer are watchers, edge-dwellers.

*DM notes: Mistpelt may be seen at the edges of things — the tree line, the river's bend — before she is ever approached. An encounter that requires patience and approaching without threat.*

---

## Glintleap — The Frog

**Human name:** Unknown.
**Nature:** Quick, slippery, at home at the water's edge.

*DM notes: Glintleap could live near the Willow River. An encounter for a summer session — wading in water, something requiring quick thinking and adaptability.*

---

## Flicker — The Mouse

**Human name:** Unknown.
**Nature:** Small. Often overlooked. Notices everything.

*DM notes: Flicker may be hiding in plain sight — in the bakery, in the general store, in someone's wall. The encounter could be quiet and intimate. Flicker sees what everyone else misses.*

---

## Skyflutter — The Butterfly

**Human name:** Unknown.
**Nature:** Transformation. Emergence. The last one to be found may be the first one to have changed the most.

*DM notes: Skyflutter could be the most transformed of all — furthest from her human origins, most purely spirit. Save this encounter for when the players are ready for something philosophical and strange.*

---

## Notes for Planning

- Each Guardian encounter should teach the players something — about themselves, about the world, about each other.
- Some Guardians may be suffering, bitter, or blight-touched. Not all encounters need to go smoothly.
- Each Guardian has held something alone for centuries. Each has a cost and a wound. Approach them with that weight in mind.
- Whistlewing said some may have *"turned bitter — or been twisted by the blight."* Plan at least one difficult, ambiguous encounter.
