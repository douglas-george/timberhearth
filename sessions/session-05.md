# Session 05 — The Mole, the Cat, and the Fray

## Summary

Poodler led the way — into a tunnel below the village, down to Gnarltooth, and back up through the most chaotic bakery morning in Timberhearth's history. Gnarltooth gave Poodler eight minutes of speech. Poodler used them. Glowfern assessed them, found them acceptable, and gave Jessica a silver whisker. Then Vendraxis struck the village directly — and the fountain fell.

---

## Events

### The Hatch

Poodler had been acting strangely — staring at certain patches of earth, spinning in tight circles, sitting on things no one else noticed. In a clearing south of the village, he stopped at a rusted metal ring half-buried in the dirt and sat on it with his entire weight.

*"I think he wants us to open it,"* Jessica said.

*"Or he's just really into cold dirt,"* Gabriel said.

The hatch groaned open. Below: a narrow stone tunnel, roots dangling from the ceiling, and a faint green glow in the depths. Poodler bolted inside.

*"We're following him, aren't we?"* Gabriel said.

*"Of course we are."*

### Gnarltooth

The tunnels were old — older than Timberhearth, older than Hollowroot. Runes on the walls. Whispers when you passed too close. Poodler stopped to bark at a wall, licked a mushroom that made him sneeze three times, and presented Gabriel with a serious piece of bark that turned out not to be magical. He wagged his tail anyway.

The tunnel opened into a wide chamber: soft moss, pulsing crystals, massive roots twisted overhead. And in the center, a massive mole carving something into stone with slow, careful movements.

*"You brought the loud one,"* Gnarltooth said, nodding at Poodler. *"That must mean it's serious."*

He retrieved the magical vest from a stone chest — silver-threaded, rune-embroidered, fitted with a glowing cravat. The dressing of Poodler required two muffins, a piece of jerky, and a solemn promise from Gabriel never to brush Poodler's fur again. The vest was fastened.

### Poodler Speaks

The moment it clicked closed, Poodler sat bolt upright:

*"HELLO! I HAVE OPINIONS AND YOU'RE GOING TO HEAR THEM!"*

Eight minutes followed. Key disclosures included: the scroll tasted like moldy socks and ambition; the rock he barked at was a case of mistaken identity; he did eat the bacon behind the stove and would do it again; the vest was incredible; he was uncertain about arms but optimistic; and he wished to be remembered as he was, not as he barked.

When the vest fizzled, he scratched his ear and licked Gabriel's shoe.

### Gnarltooth's Quiet Admission

Jessica asked, quietly, if he wanted to reconnect.

*"I think about it all the time."*

He had been awkward and loud and too strange. He dug down. He thought maybe he was better off alone. *"But lately… the silence is louder than it used to be."*

Poodler rested his head on the mole's knee. *"You smell like belonging."*

Gnarltooth gave a single nod. *"Maybe you could take a message back. Just to start."*

### The Great Cat Calamity

Back in the village: Jessica wrote a sign for the bakery window — *Wanted: One Cat for Adoption.* Poodler was tasked with hanging it. Poodler's tail caught the inkpot. By the time it reached the window, the sign read: **Wanted: EVERY Cat for Adoption.**

By evening, the bakery had cats. By morning, it had all of them.

Kittens swung from bread racks. Three flour-dusted cats turned the mixing bowl into a snowstorm. An orange kitten guarded the cash box. Two black kittens wrestled in the croissants. Poodler sat in the middle with five kittens hanging from his tail.

Maribel emerged from the back room holding her rolling pin like a weapon.

### Glowfern

One cat was not playing.

A sleek silver cat sat on the windowsill, perfectly still, tail curled, green eyes following every movement with calm authority. When she flicked her tail, the kittens paused. When she turned her head, they moved aside.

She led them to a small sunlit courtyard. Then, without moving her mouth:

*"You walk like unbalanced chairs."*

She introduced herself: **Glowfern. Guardian of Poise, Keeper of Sunbeams, Napper of the Fifth Hour, and occasional savior of small towns infested with mice and mediocrity.**

Three trials followed. Gabriel crashed into cabbages. Jessica was distracted by a kitten mid-barrel-balance. They waited twenty minutes for an invisible mouse that turned out to be a magic shimmer. They brushed every kitten in the courtyard while Glowfern presided smugly.

*"Yes. You'll do."*

She dropped a silver whisker at Jessica's feet. *"A gift. Use it wisely — or humorously. Either is fine."*

Then she vanished to a rooftop.

### The Great Pumpkin Fray

On a chilly evening shortly after, Vendraxis struck directly. Creeping Wither pumpkins erupted from cracks in the cobblestones and burst from the soil at the square's edges — glowing, vine-wrapped, awake with malevolent purpose. They moved through the streets, crawled over fences, crept toward houses and the marketplace.

Gabriel, Jessica, and Poodler drove them back. Jessica used her nature magic to slow the advancing vines. Gabriel kept the pumpkins pushed away from the homes. Poodler chased down every stray gourd he could reach.

They stopped most of it. But the town fountain — the sparkling centerpiece of the square — was struck by a vine burst of unusual strength. It shattered.

The village was safe. The fountain was gone.

- Followed Poodler into the unknown tunnel.
- Accepted Gnarltooth's hesitant offer of reconnection and agreed to carry his message.
- Fought back during the Pumpkin Fray rather than retreating.
