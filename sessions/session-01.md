# Session 01 — The Night of Voices

## Summary

On a crisp autumn evening, the village of Timberhearth gathered beneath the clock tower for the Night of Voices — the sacred tradition in which each sixteen-year-old climbs to meet Whistlewing and receives his guidance. Gabriel and Jessica had anticipated this night for as long as they could remember. What they did not anticipate was being called together.

---

## Events

### The Ceremony Begins

The village square shimmered with a hundred lanterns. The scent of cinnamon, roasted chestnuts, and fresh bread filled the air. Maribel Willowglen stood behind the bakery stall; Thomas and Elaina Thatcher stood with quiet pride in the crowd. The families had given their gifts that morning — 65 coalmarks to Gabriel, 250 to Jessica.

Mayor Kempthorne addressed the village from the clock tower steps in his burgundy coat and gold-trimmed sash. As the ceremony began, youths ascended one by one: Elaina Tussle, Rowan Fairbairn. Each returned changed in some small, subtle way.

Then the voice came again — louder this time. *"Gabriel Thatcher. Jessica Willowglen."*

The crowd stirred. *"Together?"* No two youths had ever been called at once in all of Timberhearth's memory.

Mayor Kempthorne's brow furrowed ever so slightly. *"You're called,"* he said softly.

Gabriel and Jessica stepped forward together.

### The Climb

Inside the tower: a spiral staircase lit by faintly glowing stones. With each step, the world below slipped away. Carvings on the walls — stars, feathers, shapes that shifted if you looked too long. A deep hoot echoed from above.

*"Ready?"* Gabriel whispered at the final landing.

*"No,"* Jessica said.

*"Me neither. Let's go anyway."*

### The Meeting

The chamber at the top: circular, open to the sky, filled with feathers and glowing stones and the whisper of unseen winds. And at the center, on a worn timber beam: Whistlewing. Nearly two feet tall. Silver feathers. Eyes like molten gold.

His voice came not from his beak but from within — heard not by ears, but by the heart.

*"You have come. Together. Rare… but not unwelcome."*

He listened to them speak — not of what others believed about them, but of what they knew, deep within. Then he drew two objects from within his feathers.

**To Jessica:** A stone leaf, delicate and lace-like, carved as if by water and time. *"This will remind you that even the smallest seed holds the power to grow, to heal, to endure."*

**To Gabriel:** A dark crystal in the shape of a heart, faintly luminous, as though lit from within. *"It holds the power to bring people together. And yet, before you can use it well, you must first learn to gather the pieces of yourself."*

He also told them that he had seen signs — shadows where none should be, the faint scent of magic curling through the square. He asked them to walk the town, watch, and return to him in seven days.

*"The world you know is not the whole world. Soon, it will begin to open. Be kind to the story that follows you."*

### Afterward

As they returned to the square, they felt the weight of the town's eyes — curious, cautious, awed. Whistlewing had seen something in them. Timberhearth itself seemed to sense that something new had begun.

## Key Choices Made

- Accepted Whistlewing's request to investigate the town and return in seven days.
