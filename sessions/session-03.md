# Session 03 — Secrets and Trust

## Summary

Gabriel and Jessica returned to the alley — this time they didn't run. They chose to follow the raccoons rather than the mysterious rooftop figure, and met the Ringtailed Ringleader face-to-face. Jessica preserved his sigil mark. They confided in Maribel, who revealed she already knew more than she'd said. At the Willow River, Orrin told them everything. And at the edge of town, they found the corrupted pumpkin patch — where Ringtail saved their lives, warned them the seal was failing, and collapsed.

---

## Events

### The Alley — Second Visit

This time: lantern out, instinct forward. The raccoons were there again — moving in eerie synchrony. And in the center of the troop, a cloaked raccoon in a ragged reddish-brown cloak clasped with a glinting metal charm. The Ringtailed Ringleader.

*"That one... that's the Ringtailed Ringleader,"* Jessica breathed.

Above on the rooftop: a cloaked human figure, scarfed, pausing just long enough for the lantern light to catch an edge of fabric — then gone.

**The choice:** pursue the figure or follow the raccoons.

They followed the raccoons.

### The Touch and the Sigil

In the alley, Gabriel stepped forward and gently tapped the Ringleader with a small stick — not as a threat, but as contact.

The raccoon *grabbed* the stick. Not yanked — grabbed. He held Gabriel's gaze. In his eyes: recognition. Not animal instinct. Something deeper.

Then he threw the stick down hard, let out a hiss that echoed down the alley, and vanished over the wall.

Where he had stood, the bricks were still warm. A spiral-shaped claw mark had been seared into the stone.

Jessica pressed a piece of bakery bread to the hot brick. Not a clean impression — but a faint charred spiral. Evidence. She kept it.

### Maribel's Kitchen

They told Maribel everything. When they mentioned the Ringtailed Ringleader, she gasped — softly, but genuinely. She turned away to steady herself.

She had been meeting with **Orrin** in secret for weeks — Gabriel's younger brother, coming to her kitchen door with questions about raccoons and dreams. She had chosen to believe him. She had kept his confidence.

She didn't tell them about Orrin. Not yet. But she said: *"I think something old is stirring. And I think you're part of the reason it's waking up."*

### Orrin at the River

At a family gathering by the Willow River — patchwork quilts, honey, melon, the adults swapping stories — Gabriel and Jessica found Orrin sitting apart on a flat rock, a pebble in his hand.

*"They come to my window,"* he said, without looking up. *"Sometimes just one or two. Sometimes a whole bunch. They scratch at the glass or leave things — twigs in weird shapes, knots of string, even a watch once. That's how I know it's time to go."*

He followed them. Ringtail would *show* him things — not with words, but with images, instinct. The spreading wrongness. The cracks in the air. He tried to draw the town's attention through mischief. It mostly made people annoyed.

*"I'm glad it's not just me anymore,"* he said.

### The Pumpkin Patch

That night, following whispers and wind — no lantern this time — they found it past a crooked overgrown gate at the edge of town.

A pumpkin patch. But not like any patch they'd known. The pumpkins glowed faintly — not with light but with *attention*. They turned toward the newcomers as if watching. At the center, a broken scarecrow with vines threading its limbs, and Ringtail's spiral sigil burned into the post behind it.

*"We need to go,"* Gabriel said.

Then the nearest pumpkin shuddered. Then another. The whole patch began to move.

A vine lashed out and caught Gabriel's boot. He fell.

**Then: light.** A burst of green-gold radiance shattered the darkness. Ringtail — cloak torn, fur soot-stained — drove back the vine with a hiss like steam. He raised both paws. A ring of blazing sigils blazed into the earth around the players.

*"Tell Whistlewing. The seal is failing. The blight is awake."*

Another vine. Ringtail caught it, flung it aside — then dropped to one knee.

Gabriel moved toward him. *"We can help—"*

Ringtail looked up. Pain in his eyes. And peace.

*"You already are."*

The sigil ring burst into a column of light. The patch recoiled. Ringtail collapsed.

Gabriel and Jessica ran — back toward the clock tower, back toward the one who might hold the answers.

## Key Choices Made

- Chose to follow the raccoons rather than pursue the rooftop figure. (The figure remains unidentified.)
- Decided to preserve Ringtail's sigil mark — physical evidence of something real.
- Confided fully in Maribel.
