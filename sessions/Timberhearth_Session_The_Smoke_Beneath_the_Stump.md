# Timberhearth

## Session Title: *The Smoke Beneath the Stump*

### A Tale of Division, Courage, and Two Sparks Aligned

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Opening Atmosphere

The evening in Timberhearth began wrong.

Not with storm or shadow, but with stillness. The kind that presses
gently against the ears. The kind that makes even the leaves seem to
wait.

Gabriel and Jessica stood near Mossel's infamous stump --- now reduced
to a wide, hollow wound in the earth. From its center, a thin ribbon of
purple smoke curled upward, carrying with it the faint scent of
cinnamon... and burnt pumpkin seeds.

Across town, a shout split the air:

> "THE PUMPKINS ARE MOVING AGAIN!"

A cart overturned.

A chicken screamed with theatrical intensity.

And from the western fields came a deep, rolling tremor.

Ringtail appeared on a rooftop, silhouetted in the fading light. He
tapped his chest twice and pointed --- not toward the fields --- but
toward the smoking stump.

Whistlewing descended heavily onto a fencepost.

> "You cannot do both."

The ground shook harder.

And the choice came.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Division

They split.

Gabriel ran west toward the fields.

Jessica descended into the stump.

The tremor deepened as they separated.

Far below, something ancient whispered:

> "They are divided."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Gabriel --- The Fields of Chaos

The pumpkin patch was no longer a patch.

It writhed.

Vines slithered like serpents through churned soil. Pumpkins rolled and
collided. One wore the Mayor's hat and headbutted a scarecrow. Another
sprouted three glowing eyes and hissed with hollow fury.

At the center rose a massive pumpkin --- wagon-sized --- its surface
etched with the same symbol Ringtail had scratched in the alley. The
Creeping Wither's mark.

It turned.

It saw Gabriel.

And for a moment --- he froze.

Children stood frozen by the fence.

Ivan flailed at a vine with a broom.

The giant pumpkin began uprooting itself.

Gabriel could not be everywhere.

The dark crystal heart in his pocket grew warm.

He closed his eyes.

> "Help us."

The crystal pulsed.

Not explosively.

Not violently.

But steadily.

And something shifted.

The panic did not vanish --- but it unified.

Ivan looked to the children.

The Mayor shouted:

> "FORM A LINE!"

Farmers grabbed rakes. Bakers hurled baskets. Adults locked arms and
seized vines together.

The giant pumpkin no longer faced one boy.

It faced a town.

When Gabriel sensed the purple energy pulsing rhythmically through the
carved symbol, he moved.

He disrupted its rhythm with the crystal's counter-pulse.

The energy faltered.

He climbed.

Leapt from cart to fence to ridged pumpkin hide.

And struck the glowing symbol directly.

The stick shattered.

The symbol fractured.

The purple energy imploded inward.

The massive pumpkin crashed into its own field.

The smaller pumpkins fell lifeless.

The Mayor retrieved his hat.

Ivan stared at Gabriel.

> "You are banned from normal chores."

But beneath their feet...

The ground trembled again.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Jessica --- Beneath the Seal

The spiral stair beneath the stump descended into ancient stone.

Runes carved into the walls depicted animal forms: owl, raccoon, fox,
badger --- and others unrecognized.

At the chamber's center stood a pillar bearing thirteen animal carvings
spiraled upward.

One carving glowed steady (owl).

One flickered faintly (raccoon).

One was cracked.

From shadow emerged a figure of root and stone dust.

Tall. Thin. Ancient.

> "Completion," it rasped.

It spoke of forgotten rules.

Of broken tradition.

Of division feeding awakening.

Jessica asked what would happen if the seal failed.

The vision came:

Timberhearth overrun by endless vines.\
Pumpkins towering like houses.\
Roots splitting streets.\
Sky dimmed by creeping growth.

> "Growth," it whispered.\
> "Abundance."

Jessica did not bargain.

She placed her hand over the cracked carving.

Released her Moonspark.

The golden warmth spread like rising bread.

Like a hearth catching flame.

Purple energy resisted.

The creature whispered:

> "You cannot mend what is forgotten."

But she thought of the bakery.\
Of her mother listening.\
Of Gabriel standing alone in chaos.

The crack sealed halfway.

Then further.

The pillar stabilized.

Golden light spread.

The creature recoiled.

> "One spark does not end growth."

The chamber began collapsing.

She ran.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Convergence

Above, Gabriel saw the earth sinking near the stump.

He ran.

Jessica burst from the collapsing stair as stone slabs fell behind her.

A massive chunk of stone dropped where she had stood.

The ground slid.

They slid with it.

Gabriel lunged and caught her arm.

Ringtail leapt onto his back.

Whistlewing slammed down beside them, wings braced wide against the
sliding earth.

Together, they held.

The sinkhole partially collapsed inward, sealing much of the chamber
below.

Dust settled.

Silence returned.

The golden glow pulsed faintly beneath the soil.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Aftermath

The fields lay quiet.

The pumpkins were inert.

The stump was half-collapsed but no longer smoking.

Whistlewing regarded them carefully.

> "You chose division."

A pause.

> "And yet... you returned."

Ringtail stared into the sealed earth.

> "The seal holds --- for now."

From below, faint and distant:

A low rumble.

Not gone.

Not finished.

But delayed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Themes of the Session

-   Hesitation carries consequence.\
-   Unity requires leadership.\
-   Mending is harder than striking.\
-   Division feeds awakening --- but reconciliation strengthens the
    seal.\
-   Two sparks aligned can stabilize what one alone cannot.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Ending Image

As dusk settled over Timberhearth:

The Mayor hurried toward them with questions.

Ivan whispered about "hero paperwork."

The wind stirred lightly through the damaged fields.

And beneath the soil ---

Faint.

Slow.

Waiting.

A pulse answered.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**End of Session**
