# The Seal of Hollowroot — The Covenant of Binding

*Encountered by the players as a fragment from the Tome of Hollowroot, dated Year 1 of the Quiet Age.*

---

> *"Let this be written in stone and spirit, that the story of our fall and our rising not be lost."*

Seven hundred twenty-two years ago, long before Timberhearth was carved from pine and stone, this land was known as Hollowroot Vale. Its rivers ran sweet, its orchards bent low with fruit, and its people lived by kinship rather than coin.

Among those people lived two souls of particular note. **Elric Brightfeather**, a scholar-clockmaker whose tower sang with soft bells — gentle, meticulous, beloved by all who sought wisdom. And **Kaelen of the Glades**, a half-wild youth raised more by raccoons than men — dirt on his cheeks, mischief in his smile, stealing only what others wasted and giving away more than he kept.

When the change came, it came quietly.

A shadow fell across the eastern hills — not from the sun, but from the soil. Pumpkins grew bloated and twisted. Their vines burrowed deep, coiling with strange intelligence. Those who ate the seeds found dreams not their own creeping into their minds. A man vanished, leaving behind only a patch of slick rot where he'd slept.

Elric was the first to suspect. Kaelen was the first to see. And both agreed — they could not face this alone.

---

## The Thirteen Who Stood

Elric and Kaelen gathered others — leaders not of armies, but of life:

- **Mira Cinderglen**, whose bakery hearth warmed half the Vale. She saw the rot in her flour.
- **Bramm the Forge-Hand**, who shaped sigils into steel. His anvil cracked without cause.
- **Sister Aldith**, veiled and soft-spoken, who read the moon and dreamed of vines choking the stars.
- **Torren Wex**, a carpenter with laughter in his lungs. His ladders snapped overnight.
- **Garron Holt**, the gruff old miner, who hadn't come to a feast in decades — but appeared at the Hollowroot Tree and stood firm.
- **Thalia Grimbrow**, the boisterous tavern-keeper who once arm-wrestled a bear (or so she claimed). She poured the circle's first cup of binding mead.
- And six others: **Mistpelt**, **Glintleap**, **Flicker**, **Skyflutter**, **Glowfern**, and one more whose name is not recorded in this fragment.

**Thirteen came. Thirteen stood.**

---

## The Covenant

To banish Vendraxis was impossible. But to bind him — that could be done. At terrible cost.

The **Covenant of Binding** required the surrender of form. Each of them would give up their humanity, assuming the spirit of a creature aligned to their essence. They would become the **Guardians Eternal** — neither beast nor mortal — watching over the prison for as long as the seal held.

- **Elric** became **Whistlewing**, the great owl.
- **Kaelen** took the form of **Ringtail**, the raccoon.
- **Mira's** warmth burned into **Embermane**, the fox whose fire never dies.
- **Bramm** became **Stoneback**, the unyielding badger.
- **Sister Aldith** took the wings of **Mothshade**, drifting between waking and dream.
- **Torren** became **Hearthgnaw**, the bold and acrobatic squirrel.
- **Garron** descended into the soil as **Gnarltooth**, the mole.
- **Thalia** transformed into **Bramblegut**, the great boar — her laughter now an echo through the roots.
- And five others became: **Mistpelt** the deer, **Glintleap** the frog, **Flicker** the mouse, **Skyflutter** the butterfly, and **Glowfern** the cat.

---

## The Condition

At the center of the ritual stood the pumpkin — twisted and corrupted. It became both key and lock. To anchor the spell, a tradition was seeded into the people of Hollowroot:

> **Never eat pumpkin seeds.**

For within each one, a fragment of Vendraxis still slumbered. The act of eating one would be to invite him back, piece by piece.

The Vale's people were told this truth. But as generations passed, the memory dulled. Warnings became folklore. Folklore became superstition. And superstition became jokes.

> *"They cause warts." "They attract goblins." "My uncle ate one and turned orange."*

But the truth sleeps beneath their feet.
