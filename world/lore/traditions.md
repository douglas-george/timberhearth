# Traditions of Timberhearth

---

## The Night of Voices

✅ **[CANON]** The **Night of Voices** is Timberhearth's most sacred annual tradition. Once a year, when the autumn lanterns are lit and the village square fills with the scent of honey cakes and roasted chestnuts, the sixteen-year-olds of the town gather to climb the clock tower and meet **Whistlewing**.

The invitation is never delivered directly. Instead, the elders begin to *happen* to mention the tower in the same breath as the youth's name. Mossel sweeps the cobblestones more pointedly. Someone says, with studied casualness: *"Well. Whistlewing will be wanting a word."*

No one ever speaks of what Whistlewing tells them. Some return beaming. Others look thoughtful, or worried. But they never tell.

✅ **[CANON]** Families present their sixteen-year-olds with a gift of **coalmarks** the morning of the ceremony — a traditional coming-of-age offering representing independence and the beginning of one's chosen trade.

---

## Coalmarks

✅ **[CANON]** **Coalmarks** are the currency of Timberhearth. Practical, tactile, and hard-earned.

Notable amounts in the campaign:
- Gabriel received **65 coalmarks** from his family at the Night of Voices.
- Jessica received **250 coalmarks** from the Willowglen Bakery family — reflecting their prosperity and high expectations.
- Gabriel paid **50 coalmarks** for a lantern at Ivan Ranger's General Store.
- Gabriel left **200 coalmarks** with Mossel as collateral on their stump-clearing deal.

## The Pumpkin Seed Taboo

👁️ **[REVEALED]** No one in Timberhearth eats pumpkin seeds. The reasons people give vary wildly:

> *"They cause bad dreams." "They'll make your tongue turn orange forever." "Eating them makes you forget your name." "They'll summon raccoons to your house." "If you eat one, you'll never find true love." "Pumpkin seeds are witch bait."*

The children of Timberhearth dare each other to crack seeds open behind the bakery at night — though no one ever actually eats one.

The tradition is also echoed in:
- **Autumn songs** with cryptic verses about "never swallowing the seed."
- A **superstition** that a pumpkin seed landing upright brings good luck — but only if you spit it.
- A **harvest ritual** where seeds are buried in the woods and never spoken of again.
- An unenforced **town ordinance** banning the sale of roasted pumpkin seeds in shops.

👁️ **[REVEALED]** The players now know the true reason: the taboo is a binding thread of the seal imprisoning Vendraxis. Every seed eaten is a ripple through the prison.

## The Caretaker's Rounds

✅ **[CANON]** Mossel Crabtree tends the village square with a slow, methodical devotion — flowerbeds, squeaky gates, cobblestones, and the general dignity of Timberhearth's public spaces. His rounds are a constant background rhythm of the town. He is the one who sweeps the cobblestones more pointedly toward the sixteen-year-olds. He is not entirely comfortable with magic, but he respects tradition absolutely.

---

## The Harvest Council

👁️ **[REVEALED]** At sixteen, Timberhearth residents gain the right to vote at the **Harvest Council** — the town's seasonal assembly where community decisions are made. This is one of the formal markers of adulthood alongside the Night of Voices ceremony.

---

## The Willow River Gatherings

✅ **[CANON]** The Willowglen and Thatcher families share a tradition of gathering at the Willow River on lazy mornings — patchwork quilts under the willows, baskets of rolls and hand pies, honey, melon. A tradition as old as either family's memory. It was at such a gathering that Orrin revealed his months-long secret to Gabriel and Jessica.
