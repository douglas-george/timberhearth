# World Overview — Timberhearth

## The Town

✅ **[CANON]** Timberhearth is a small, insular village nestled between the **whispering West Woods** to the west and the **tumbling Willow River** to the east. It is the kind of place where every family knows whose roof leaks and whose boots have been patched twice — where the peculiar wears a familiar face and ordinary things carry the weight of old warnings.

The town is built around a cobblestoned **village square**, at the center of which once stood a sparkling fountain (now destroyed in the Great Pumpkin Fray). The square is anchored by the **Willowglen Bakery** on one side and the **clock tower** on the other. Surrounding the square are modest shops, homes, and gardens maintained by generations of the same families.

---

## Tone and Themes

Timberhearth is a cozy, magical, story-driven world built for collaborative discovery. Its dominant themes are:

- **Forgotten truths hidden in plain sight.** Ancient knowledge degrades into superstition, joke, and habit. The players are the ones who start to see through the surface.
- **The cost of old magic.** The world's protections were purchased at extraordinary personal sacrifice. New protections may demand the same.
- **Children and families at the center.** The stakes are human and warm before they are cosmic.
- **Gentle whimsy with real danger.** A raccoon with a cloak. A dog who speaks for eight minutes. An owl who watched a thousand ceremonies. The world is funny and sweet — and also genuinely threatened.

## Magic

👁️ **[REVEALED]** Magic in Timberhearth is called **Spark** (or Moonspark). It is rare, real, and old. The town's Wisdom, Shanna Parsnip, teaches it at the edge of the village. Magic is not inherited — it is a *quality* in a person, something to be coaxed and cultivated. Jessica has a natural affinity for nature magic. Gabriel has a gift for connection.

🔒 **[HIDDEN]** The deeper truth is that magic in this region is intimately tied to the thirteen Guardians and the seal. As the seal weakens, wild magic becomes more volatile and blight-touched magic grows stronger. True mastery of Spark may eventually require understanding its relationship to the Covenant of Binding.

---

## The Pumpkin Seed Ban

👁️ **[REVEALED]** No one in Timberhearth eats pumpkin seeds. No law enforces it — but raised eyebrows, sharp coughs from aunties, and the weight of generations make the taboo feel absolute. People give dozens of different reasons:

> *"They cause warts." "They attract goblins." "My uncle ate one and turned orange."*

The players now know the true reason: the ban is a **binding thread** of the magical seal that imprisons Vendraxis. Each seed eaten sends a ripple through the seal. As the tradition has weakened, Vendraxis has stirred.

---

## The Wider World

🔒 **[HIDDEN]** Timberhearth is small and self-contained, but the land around it — once known as **Hollowroot Vale** — has a long and layered history. The seal's roots extend beneath the entire valley. The eight undiscovered Guardians are scattered: some in the woods, some hidden in plain sight in the town itself, some perhaps in places beyond Timberhearth's edge.
